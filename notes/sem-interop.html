Semantic Interoperability and Discourse

April 2020

Syntactic, structural, semantic interop (classification by Amit Sheth). Isn't what we've all been doing for the past 20y? Semantic interop may be nonesensical if we think of it: languages are not meant to be "executed" but understood/interpreted. A schema, a piece of code, a specification is really like an utterance, a speech act: it was adressed to another agent with some intent. Trying to make two agent interoperate, given that their mutual utterances are mutually interpreted the wrong way, is probably not going to be solved by a third person telling them something. It is like sparkling a fight. Authoritarian interoperability. Alternative: conversational interop? How to Do Things with Words (Austin).
