Semantic Interoperability and Discourse (II)

April 2022

Introduction to semantic interoperability given at a winter school (pdf). Mostly made of references commonly accepted in the SOTA. But intuition SOTA isn't targeting the right problems: aren't all forms of interoperabiltiy necessarily semantic?

Interoperability: exchange of information between independent systems. Examples: IFC (for construction data exchange), CISE (for international maritime surveillance).

Moving from interop to semantic interop: moving from XSD to OWL. What for? Mostly for stating e.g. MyCompanyClass subClassOf CommonClass.

To what extent is subsumption used/useful? Without OWL, the emitter is responsible for projecting its data onto a common model and the receiver is responsible for doing the opposite transformation. With OWL, the emitter is responsible instead for projecting its data model onto the common model. The receiver then is responsible for making the alignment but it still has to project the data represented in a common model onto its own.

Commonality between IFC and CISE: both names and initial design suggest a much wider scope than what they are effectively used for (construction vs. entire industry, maritime surveillance vs. all government regulation activity).

See also Semantic Interoperability and Discourse (April 2020).
