On the Power of Knowledge

March 2020

Why would one want to structure Wikipedia into something like Wikidata? Quite simply: because it is easier to act on it. Knowing something generally implies the action of whoever possesses the knowledge is partially conditioned by that knowledge. Conversely, doing something implies the prior existence of some form of knowledge. Now, when knowledge is ground into real-valued vectors (aka embeddings), the relationship between knowing and doing is not expressed in terms of logical necessity (or probable necessity) but in "geometric" terms. The theory of relativity also gives a geometric interpretation of things by expressing physical events in a single spacetime continuum. (The analogy reveals the importance of an observer, i.e. of a basis that every coordinate system requires.) The behavior of agents generally being specified in terms of external stimuli and internal drives, the question of "acting" on knowledge is that of specifying geometric transformations. Similarly, causality is expressed as equations between measurable quantities within the vector space.
